# Key Drivers Tone Guide

This guide ensures that **key_drivers** explanations are human-readable, clear, and avoid technical jargon.

## Core Principles

1. **Write for a high school audience** — anyone should understand why a job is or isn't resilient to AI
2. **Focus on the human reality** — not the scoring mechanics
3. **Avoid all attribute notation** — no A1, A2, "(A3=4)" references
4. **Explain the "why"** — not just list factors

---

## Examples

### ✅ GOOD: Clear, Human-Centered

**Job:** Mental Health Counselor
**Score:** 4.7

> The therapeutic relationship is the intervention—clients seek a specific person for their perspective, empathy, and presence. The role requires constant judgment in complex, unpredictable situations (assessing risk, choosing treatment approaches). Licensed accountability means society legally requires a qualified human in charge. The work also benefits from AI: paperwork and treatment research can be compressed, freeing counselors to focus on actual care.

**Why this works:**
- Explains what makes the job hard to automate (relationship, judgment, legal requirement)
- Mentions the opportunity (AI for admin work)
- Uses plain language
- No technical references

---

**Job:** Nurse Anesthetist
**Score:** 4.4

> Anesthesia demands real-time judgment in high-stakes situations—monitoring vital signs, responding instantly to complications, adapting dosage to individual physiology. Patients and surgeons trust a credentialed professional with their life. Deep knowledge of drugs, patient history, and institutional protocols is built over years. These legal and relational demands won't disappear, and anesthesia will remain a bottleneck that creates demand for skilled practitioners.

**Why this works:**
- Concrete (monitoring vitals, adjusting dosage)
- Emphasizes irreplaceability (legal requirement, time-built expertise)
- No jargon or attribute names

---

### ❌ BAD: Technical, Attribute-Heavy

**DON'T write this:**

> A1 and A3 scores are high, contributing to strong defensive positioning. A4 provides additional protection through licensure. A9 opportunities exist but are secondary. Overall resilience is driven by A1 + A3 + A4 > A6 + A7.

**Why this fails:**
- Readers don't know what A1, A3, A4 mean
- Focuses on the scoring system, not the actual work
- Unreadable for anyone not trained on the framework
- Treats the job as a formula, not a human role

---

**DON'T write this:**

> High A2 score (4) and medium A5 (3) combine with low A6 (2), resulting in a defensive composite of 3.5.

**Why this fails:**
- Completely opaque without the scoring rubric
- Numbers are meaningless to the reader
- Ignores the human context

---

### 🎯 REWRITE EXAMPLES

**Original (bad):**
> A1=5 (physical presence in unstructured environment), A3=4 (novel judgment), A4=4 (legal accountability). These defensives offset A6=2 (limited political navigation).

**Rewritten (good):**
> The work happens in unpredictable, physical environments where a human must be present and make split-second decisions. Legal responsibility means a licensed person must be in charge. These barriers won't disappear even as AI improves.

---

**Original (bad):**
> Moderate A9 (3.0) suggests some administrative compression possible. A5 (4.0) provides deep organizational knowledge defense.

**Rewritten (good):**
> Years of organizational knowledge about how systems actually work—not just in theory, but in practice—is hard to replace. There's potential for AI to handle routine paperwork, freeing up time for higher-value work.

## Checklist for Key Drivers

Before submitting a key_drivers response, verify:

- [ ] No attribute names (A1-A10)
- [ ] No scores in parentheses or brackets
- [ ] No terms like "defensive," "offensive," "weighted"
- [ ] Written in plain English, no technical jargon
- [ ] Explains why the job resists or invites automation
- [ ] Focuses on the actual work, not the framework
- [ ] 2-3 sentences, clear and concrete
- [ ] A high school student would understand the explanation

---

## Length & Structure

**Ideal length:** 2-3 sentences (about 60-90 words)

**Structure:**
1. **Why it's protected** (1 sentence): What makes automation hard?
2. **Additional factor** (1 sentence): A secondary source of resilience or vulnerability?
3. **Opportunity or threat** (1 sentence, optional): What changes this calculus?

**Example:**

> Dentists must be physically present to perform intricate procedures in a patient's mouth—something no robot can do reliably today. Patients also trust a licensed professional with their health. The profession benefits from AI handling administrative work and initial diagnostics, which could expand the dentist's capacity to focus on complex cases.

---

## Context for Scorers

Remember: **Key drivers are for the end user, not for the data team.**

The general public will read these. They should:
- Make sense without training on the framework
- Give genuine insight into why a job is resilient
- Avoid sounding like algorithm output
- Feel human, specific, and grounded

If your key drivers could be swapped between two jobs, they're too generic. Make them **specific to this role's actual work.**
